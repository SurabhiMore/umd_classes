# Static Decision-Making

Static decision-making refers to one-time, offline decisions where you have a well-defined problem, sufficient data or domain knowledge, and a clear decision to make without sequential feedback or adaptation. Unlike sequential decision-making (where you adapt based on outcomes), static decisions are made once with the best information available at the time.
