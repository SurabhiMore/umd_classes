# Sequential Decision-Making

Sequential decision-making combines reinforcement learning with causal structure to enable agents to learn optimal policies through repeated interactions with an environment. Unlike static decisions, sequential decisions adapt based on feedback and outcomes, integrating both learning from data and causal reasoning about intervention effects.

## Markov Decision Processes

- States, actions, rewards, and transitions
- Discount factors and long-term value
- Optimal policies and value functions

## Exploration vs. Exploitation Trade-Off

- Regret bounds and optimal exploration strategies
- Thompson sampling and Bayesian approaches
- Contextual bandits

## Model-Based vs. Model-Free RL

- Value iteration and policy iteration
- Q-learning and temporal difference methods
- Actor-critic methods

## Offline Reinforcement Learning and Batch Policy Evaluation

- Learning from fixed datasets without environment interaction
- Off-policy corrections and importance weighting
- Batch policy evaluation

## Causal Reinforcement Learning

- Integrating causal models into decision-making
- Causal RL: combining causal inference with policy learning
- Decision diagrams and influence diagrams
- Policy interventions and treatment policies

## Heterogeneous Treatment Effects and Personalization

- Conditional average treatment effects (CATE)
- Meta-learners for heterogeneous effects
- Personalized policy optimization
